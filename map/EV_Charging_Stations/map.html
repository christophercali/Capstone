The Charge Forward

To better understand the EV charging landscape in NYC, I bring together Department of Energy Data on publicly available EV charging stations and American Community Survey data (5-Year: Estimate of Median Household Income, Race Breakdown, and Education Level). With this information, we can identify areas that are underserved by EV charging infrastructure and make potential recommendations for equitable development.

A Demographic Lens

Takeaways and Further Questions

Outer Boroughs

Into the outer boroughs, there are fewer options for public charging available. These areas make it difficult for those interested in switching to an EV.

Income

As expected, higher income areas have more charging infrastructure. EVs are still not very affordable.

Racial Disparity

Areas with a high percentage of BIPOC (Black, Indigenous, People of Color) individuals do not have EV charging locations. What can the City do to increase equity on this front?

Education

The map for education and income do not look very different, likely because there is a high correlation between the two.
